About Me

I am a cybersecurity researcher, educator, and practitioner based in New York City. I am completing my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and Information Security at Bronx Community College, CUNY, with an expected graduation in May 2026.

As an Adjunct Lecturer at BCC, I teach five courses covering network fundamentals, switching and routing, network operating systems, cybersecurity, and ethical hacking and penetration testing. I design all course labs and assessments independently.

My research at John Jay College of Criminal Justice, CUNY, under the supervision of Prof. Fatma Najar, focuses on AI-powered deepfake detection. I developed two systems — SuGuard, a four-modality neurosymbolic pipeline, and DeepGuard, a visual classifier — both achieving around 98% accuracy. A research paper on cross-dataset generalization is currently being prepared for submission to IEEE Access.

I hold an AAS in Cybersecurity and Networking, the Carl Polowczyk Award, membership in the Phi Theta Kappa honor society, and professional certifications including CompTIA A+, CompTIA Linux+, and CEH v12.

Research & Projects

IEEE Access — Journal Paper (In Preparation)

Cross-Dataset Generalization in Deepfake Detection: A Comparative Study of Neurosymbolic Multimodal and Single-Modality Approaches

Co-authored with Prof. Fatma Najar. This study examines how well deepfake detection models trained on one dataset perform when tested on a completely different dataset. The key finding is that high accuracy on a training dataset does not guarantee strong performance on unseen data. SuGuard's accuracy drops from 98.25% to 48% in cross-dataset testing, while DeepGuard improves by 9.25 percentage points. Among all modalities, the physiology signal shows the highest cross-dataset stability at 153.2% retention.

Datasets: FakeAVCeleb v1.2, Celeb-DF-v2
Best Accuracy: 98.25%
Best AUC: 0.9993
Project — SuGuard (PRISM Symposium)

SuGuard: A Four-Modality Neurosymbolic Deepfake Detection Pipeline

SuGuard is a multimodal deepfake detection system that analyzes four types of signals: visual features using EfficientNet-B0, audio patterns using MFCC heuristics, transcribed speech using Whisper and DistilRoBERTa, and physiological signals using rPPG. These signals are combined through a weighted fusion formula and the system is deployed as a Flask web application with a forensic reasoning panel and a temporal analysis chart.

Accuracy: 98.25%
F1 Score: 98.27%
AUC: 0.9993
Dataset: FakeAVCeleb v1.2
GitHub: suguard-multimodal-deepfake-detection
Project — DeepGuard (OSRC)

DeepGuard: A Progressive Curriculum EfficientNet-B4 Deepfake Classifier

DeepGuard is a single-modality visual deepfake classifier built on EfficientNet-B4. It was trained using a progressive curriculum approach, starting with a small number of training videos and gradually increasing the dataset size over multiple stages. It is deployed as a Flask web application and shows that a well-trained single-modality model can match the accuracy of more complex multi-modal systems.

Accuracy: 98%
Dataset: Celeb-DF-v2
GitHub: deepguard-deepfake-detection
