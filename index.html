Cross-Dataset Generalization in Deepfake Detection

A Comparative Study of Neurosymbolic Multimodal and Single-Modality Approaches · Co-authored with Prof. Fatma Najar, John Jay College, CUNY

Designed and executed controlled experiments comparing SuGuard (4-modality neurosymbolic) and DeepGuard (single-modality EfficientNet-B4) on FakeAVCeleb, Celeb-DF-v2, and FaceForensics++ (5,000 videos). Key finding: physiology modality retains 153.2% discriminative signal cross-dataset while visual modality collapses to 4.2%.

SuGuard98.25% → 48.00% cross-dataset
DeepGuard54.75% → 64.00% cross-dataset

✅ Completed · PRISM Symposium May 2026

SuGuard — Multimodal Neurosymbolic Deepfake Detection

4-Modality Pipeline: Visual · Audio · Text · Physiology

Built a neurosymbolic deepfake detection system combining EfficientNet-B0 (visual), MFCC heuristic (audio), Whisper + DistilRoBERTa (text), and rPPG forehead ROI (physiology). Achieved 98.25% accuracy on FakeAVCeleb v1.2. Deployed with a Flask GUI featuring drag-and-drop upload, modality score cards, and neurosymbolic reasoning panel. Presented at PRISM Symposium, May 6, 2026.

🔬 Open Source · OSRC Symposium Apr 2026

DeepGuard — Single-Modality Deepfake Detector

EfficientNet-B4 · Progressive Transfer Learning · Celeb-DF-v2

Trained EfficientNet-B4 progressively on Celeb-DF-v2 through 3 stages, reaching 98.00% in-distribution accuracy. Key cross-dataset insight: single-modality visual models can generalize better (+9.25 pts) than complex multimodal fusion on unseen data. Flask web app deployed. Presented at OSRC Spring Symposium, April 29, 2026.
